Preferred CV Suffixes and Naming Conventions:

  • SB, NB, EB, WB: Under southbound, northbound, eastbound, or westbound only. This is for culverts that have an opening in the median.

  • SBR, NBR, EBR, WBR: Under southbound, northbound, eastbound, or westbound ramp only.

  • ADJ: Adjacent to the road (can add NB, SB, EB, WB to designate directional travel side of the road, e.g., NBADJ would mean the culvert is under a road adjacent to the northbound lanes)

  • CR: One end is under a County Road (can add NB, SB, EB, WB to designate directional travel side of the road, e.g., NBCR would mean the culvert runs under a county road adjacent to the northbound lanes)

  • SPC: Special notes on the location can be found in the report (catch basin, only has one open end, part of a complex system, confined space, etc.)

  • Combinations are possible: SB-SBCR, NB-NBR, EBR-EBCR, WBADJ-SPC

The INDOT retaining wall inspection process is not intended to be a thorough evaluation of a retaining wall’s overall structural integrity or geotechnical stability. Rather, the inspection is to report the apparent visible physical condition of the retaining wall and its components, its drainage system as applicable, and the ground and embankment conditions above the retained height and along the exposed face of the wall.


The retaining wall inspector shall provide condition ratings for both the retaining wall as a whole and its components, collect photographs, measurements, and inspection notes documenting both the overall typical conditions and suspect areas exhibiting deterioration or issues, report maintenance needs and recommendations, and provide a general recommendation of whether the wall condition warrants a more thorough investigation by retaining wall specialists.
